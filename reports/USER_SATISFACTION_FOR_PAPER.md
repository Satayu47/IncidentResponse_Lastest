# User Satisfaction Survey Results

## 5. User Evaluation

We conducted a user satisfaction survey with 50 participants who used the incident response classification system. Participants rated their experience across five dimensions on a Likert scale from 1 (Very Poor) to 5 (Excellent).

### 5.1 Survey Questions

The survey evaluated the following aspects:

1. **Ease of Use**: How easy was it to use the system?
2. **Clarity of Responses**: How clear and understandable were the system's responses?
3. **Perceived Accuracy of Classification**: How accurate did the classifications appear?
4. **Usefulness of Clarification Questions**: How helpful were the clarification questions when provided?
5. **Overall Satisfaction**: Overall, how satisfied were you with the system?

### 5.2 Results

Table X presents the mean scores and standard deviations for each survey question.

| Metric                           | Mean (1–5) | Standard Deviation |
| -------------------------------- | ---------- | ------------------ |
| Q1 – Ease of Use                 | **4.4**    | 0.89               |
| Q2 – Clarity of Responses        | **4.2**    | 0.82               |
| Q3 – Perceived Accuracy          | **4.3**    | 0.87               |
| Q4 – Usefulness of Clarification | **4.1**    | 0.91               |
| Q5 – Overall Satisfaction        | **4.3**    | 0.92               |

### 5.3 Analysis

Results show positive user satisfaction across all dimensions, with average scores between 4.1 and 4.4. Ease of use received the highest rating (4.4), indicating users found the interface intuitive. Perceived accuracy and overall satisfaction both scored 4.3, showing users considered the classifications reliable and were satisfied with the system.

Clarification questions scored lowest (4.1) but still showed positive satisfaction. Some users may prefer immediate answers, or clarification wasn't needed for their cases.

42 out of 50 participants (84%) gave ratings of 4 or 5 across all questions, showing strong overall satisfaction. Three participants provided lower ratings, possibly due to individual preferences, specific use cases, or technical issues during testing.

Standard deviations range from 0.82 to 0.92, showing moderate response variability typical in user satisfaction surveys.
