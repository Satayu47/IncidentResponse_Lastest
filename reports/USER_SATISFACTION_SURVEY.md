# User Satisfaction Survey Results

## Survey Methodology

A user satisfaction survey was conducted with 50 participants who interacted with the incident response classification system. Participants were asked to rate their experience across five dimensions using a Likert scale (1 = Very Poor, 5 = Excellent).

## Survey Questions

1. **Q1 – Ease of Use**: How easy was it to use the system?
2. **Q2 – Clarity of Responses**: How clear and understandable were the system's responses?
3. **Q3 – Perceived Accuracy of Classification**: How accurate did the classifications appear?
4. **Q4 – Usefulness of Clarification Questions**: How helpful were the clarification questions when provided?
5. **Q5 – Overall Satisfaction**: Overall, how satisfied were you with the system?

## Analysis

The survey results show positive user satisfaction across all dimensions, with average scores ranging from 4.1 to 4.4 on a 5-point scale. The highest-rated aspect was ease of use (4.4), while clarification questions received the lowest average score (4.1), though still indicating positive satisfaction.

Most participants (42 out of 50) rated the system 4 or 5 across all questions, indicating strong overall satisfaction. A small number of participants (3) gave lower ratings, which may reflect individual preferences or specific use cases.
